What is Zakat?

Zakat is a mandatory charitable giving in Islam — a portion of your zakatable wealth (typically 2.5%) given to eligible recipients (the poor, needy, etc.).

Q: What percentage is Zakat?

A: Zakat is typically 2.5% of net zakatable assets.

Q: When is Zakat due?

A: Zakat is due once a lunar year has passed on the zakatable wealth.

Q: What counts as zakatable?

A: Cash, gold, silver, business inventory and investments are typically zakatable. Personal items like your main home and personal car are usually not.
