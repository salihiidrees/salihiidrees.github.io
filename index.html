What is Zakat?

Zakat is a form of almsgiving and one of the Five Pillars of Islam. It is a religious obligation for Muslims to give a portion of their wealth to those in need.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What percentage is Zakat?
A: Zakat is typically 2.5% of your net zakatable assets.

Q: When is Zakat due?
A: Zakat is due once a year, after one lunar year has passed on your wealth.

Q: What assets are zakatable?
A: Cash, gold, silver, business inventory, and investments are zakatable. Personal items like your home and car are not.
